About Me

I am a, first-gen, Fulbright Funded Ph.D. Student in the Department of Materials Science and Nanoengineering at Rice University. As a member of of the Nanomaterials, Nanomechanics and Nanodevices Lab (N3L) supervised by Prof. Jun Lou , My research focuses on Sustainable Fuels and Clean Chemcials, with expertise on Electro/photcatalysis, Advanced Materials Synthesis and Characterizations.

Previously, I worked as a Lecturer in Dept. of Metallurgy and Materials Engineering at Dawood Unviersity of Engineering and Technology (DUET) Karachi, Pakistan. I designed and taught undergraduate courses, mentored 5+ senior-year design projects, faculty advisor to student-run chapter and contributed in departmental advancements.
I earned my Masters in Metallurgy and Materials Engineering from Mehran University of Engineering and Technology Jamshoro. AT MUET, My research was focused on developing novel graphene based nanocomposite for antibacterial applications, During my masters, I also served as a Graduate Intern at MME, MUET under National Internsip Program. I learnt advanced tools and co-supversied two undergrad research projects.
My industry experience includes working as a Trainee Engineer at a leading Cable Manufacturing Company, where I gained hands-on-expertise in melting, casting and quality control. Back in 2019, I joined DUET, as a Lab-Engineer and got promoted as Lecturer due to academic excellence and laboratory expertise.
In my free time, I enjoy exploring the worl through the eyes of my toddler, diving into thought-provoking reads, and watch TV series. I like to stay updated about world politics & technological advancements, through twitter.
